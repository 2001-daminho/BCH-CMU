    Philosophy

    The thinking and belief that guides the administration of our B Sc Biochemistry degree programme at Chrisland University, Abeokuta, in the nurturing of our students education, is that an Honours degree graduate in Biochemistry should at the end of their training be not only in full and complete command of both the theoretical and practical aspects of Biochemistry

    Objectives

    The B.Sc. degree in Biochemistry of the College of Natural and Applied Sciences is intended to be a four-year programme (8 Semesters) designed to teach the basic concepts of the chemistry of life and reasons for various life processes. The primary aim of the B.Sc Biochemistry programme is to provide the basic science of Biochemistry up to the Bachelor's Degree level.

    Entry requirements

    Candidate seeking admission into our B.Sc. Degree programme in Biochemistry must have at least five credit passes in the Senior Secondary School Certificate Examination (SSCE) or General Certificate of Education Ordinary Level (GCE "O" Level) or other government approved equivalents that must include English Language, Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics and Biology.

    Our Examination assessment methods

    consist of the written short essay and/or objective-answer question mode and a Continuous Assessment (CA) tests regimen, with marks obtainable out of 100 percent allotted in the ratio 7:3, respectively, between the written examination and the CA Test Students' Examinations question papers and proposed Marking Guides/Schemes are usually first vetted, externally, by a professor.

    The B.Sc. Degree program in Biochemistry


    It Provides students with a broad and balanced foundation of biochemical knowledge and practical skills. It aslo equips students with the ability to apply knowledge and skills at solving theoretical and practical problems in biochemistry, instilling in students a sense of enthusiasm for biochemistry, an appreciation of its application in different contexts and to involve them in an intellectually stimulating and satisfying experience of learning and studying.

    About Us

    Brief History of the Department

    The Department of Biochemistry of Chrisland University, Abeokuta, (Nigeria) is one of the academic Departments created in the College of Natural and Applied Sciences at the inception of the University in 2015/2016 academic session. It was established as a unit under the forerunner Department of Chemical Sciences, College of Natural and Applied Sciences of which Dr. O.M. Bolaji (Ph.D.) became the pioneer Head of Department in an Acting position, from 2016 to 2018. At inception the Department had only three (3) registered students. On August 15, 2018, a Chair of Biochemistry was created and Professor Emmanuel N. Maduagwu (FAS) became its first appointee and occupant. Over the years, the Department has grown by leaps and bounds and has graduated many students who are doing very well in academia, industries, research institutes and all other areas of scientific endeavors. The University graduated her 5th sets of students recently and our students from Biochemistry Department were part of this convocation ceremony. The Department currently has Professor E. Olatunde Farombi, FAS, FNSBMB, as the Head of Department.

    • The Vision of the Department is to be a world class Department of Biochemistry that is committed to a mode of teaching, learning and research training activities that constitute a structured combination of the pioneer, the new and the modern basic and practical aspects of learning objectives and outcomes in that scientific discipline.

    ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

    The normal period for an honors degree shall be eight (8) semesters. It has duration of four years for students admitted with the Ordinary Level Certificate, and three years for those with Advance Level Certificates.

    • UTME Admission

      Candidate seeking admission for B.Sc Degree in Biochemistry must have at least five credits in the Senior secondary school certificate examination (SSCE)

    • Direct Entry

      Candidates who, in addition to the five relevant credits passes at SSCE or O’Level

    UTME Admission

    Candidate seeking admission for B.Sc Degree in Biochemistry must have at least five credits in the Senior secondary school certificate examination (SSCE) including English Language, Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, and Biology at not more than two sittings. These candidates are required to answer questions in Chemistry, Biology and either Physics or Mathematics in addition to the English Language in the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examinations (UTME).

    Direct Entry

    Candidates who, in addition to the five relevant credits passes at SSCE or O’Level, with a satisfactory pass grade at A’Level in Chemistry and Physics or Mathematics, or Biology, OND Upper Credit (Science Laboratory Technology, Food Technology) may be considered for exception from the equivalent courses in the program.

    Student Body Affiliation to Learned Societies in Biochemistry

    Our students in Biochemistry Department are hereby enjoined to endeavor to seek membership of recognized learned scientific bodies, especially those cognitive with their training in biochemistry, such as the Nigerian Society of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology (NSBMB), through the Nigerian Society of Biochemistry Students (NSBS). Attendance of the annual conferences of the NSBMB will surely be rewarding.

    Student Academic Excursions

    Through the departmental effort and that of the NSBS, it should be possible to arrange excursions to sites and organizations of interest to the budding biochemist.

    Course Level Advisers

    Students should be aware of the availability of course level advisers who are in the position to guide students through their Course experiences while in the department, and through whom any complaints regarding any difficulties should first be channeled to the B.Sc. programme Coordinator for necessary attention.

    Students Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES)

    All students in the department should be aware that at a prescribed time during their training, especially during the vacation period, there will be need for them to work in other institutions and organization relevant to their training, such as the industry and private sector establishments to garner practical experience working under the tutelage of Professionals in their own field of biochemistry and cognate areas.

    Consideration and Approval of Examination Results

    Students' results are usually considered at the departmental level first, after which it will then be presented to the College Committee of Examination Results (COCER) for vetting.


    All results vetted by COCER shall normally be presented to the College Board for consideration and approval for further processing.


    The recommendations of the College Board on the results shall be presented to the Senate Business Committee for ratification and then to Senate for approval.


    The College shall normally prepare the summary of the Semester results for presentation to the Senate for consideration and approval.
